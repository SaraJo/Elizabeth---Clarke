when Raf Simons sent his models down the SS11 Jil Sander runway in glamorous floor-skimming skirts teamed with understand white tees (above), the humble white T-shirt was instantly imbued with high-fashion status

that something so simple can make such a strong statement is a testament to just how far wardrobe basics have come. women expect so much more from them today - flattering, feminine necklines, forgiving cuts, boyfriend fits, seamless silhouettes, gauzy weaves, smooth drapes, vintage washes...the list goes on

however, unlike high-end designers, we don’t spend money to build fancy stores or sell our shirts for a ridiculous mark-up to department stores. as a result, we can spend a lot more money to make high-quality shirts without increasing the price

Elizabeth & Clarke’s shirts are similar in quality to Theysken’s Theory, James Perse, T by Alexander Wang, Kain, or the Row but without the crazy mark-up
